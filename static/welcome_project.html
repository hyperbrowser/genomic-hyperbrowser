Colocalization analysis: bringing differential analysis to genome-scale omics data

A broad variety of chip- and high throughput sequencing-based methods produce sets of genomic regions as their main output. Hence, the statistical analysis of genomic colocalization is a fundamental approach for integrative genomics. Since individual genomic datasets may be affected by noise, experimental artifacts or particularities of individual donors or cell types, it would be preferable to search for signals associated with sets of related experimental datasets. However, such analyses are challenging to devise due to the many dependencies along the genome and across related genomic features, and no principled methodology is currently available for such investigations.

We here consider the question of whether two sets of genomic datasets differ significantly in their degree of colocalization with a reference genomic feature. For instance, the two sets could be two active chromatin sets, and the reference feature GWAS SNPs, so the investigation of interest would be in which chromatin set the GWAS SNPs are more active. The problem involves two main challenges; the often strong and varying degree of dependence between related (epi-)genomic features, and the general hidden confounders influencing both the expected value and variance of colocalization between datasets. We define three benchmarks that together reflect these main challenges. We propose several alternative analytical approaches and investigate their behavior on the defined benchmarks, as well as applying those methods on a real biological case. We show that a non parametric approach based on a rank-order test statistic perform adequately on all three defined benchmarks.

Case example: immune cell association with Crohn’s disease

Moreover, we applied the method that performed the best (MC3) on a previously published experiment on the co-occurrence of Crohn's disease-associated variants (GWAS SNPs) and DNase hypersensitive sites (DHSs) (published in Franke, Andre, et al. "Genome-wide meta-analysis increases to 71 the number of confirmed Crohn's disease susceptibility loci." Nature genetics 42.12 (2010): 1118.).

The data processing and the filtering of the public available data can be found at history, while the results obtained by applying MC3 for the empirical analyses of the Crohn’s disease associated variants (GWAS SNPs) and DNase hypersensitive sites (DHSs) in different cell types can be found at MC3 .

General information about the Genomic HyperBrowser

The main window (project page) is divided into three parts; the first part (on the left) shows all the tools implemented in the Genomic HyperBrowser. The tools related to this specific project are available under the label 'SIMILARITY PROJECT TOOLS'. The second part (middle) is the main resulting page. The third part (on the right) shows all the history elements of the actions made (run tools, uploaded data, etc). On each of this history element one can run at least two operations:

  1. by clicking on the icon 'eye' you can see the results of that element,
  2. by clicking on the title of the element you will see four new icons. Starting from the left you have possibility of downloading the data, viewing the details, re-running the tool and visualizing the results.
